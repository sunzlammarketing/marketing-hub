Livestreaming has become a primary sales channel for many shops. But most streams fail for the same reason: **turning on the camera and improvising**, with no script.

Viewers arrive, see nothing compelling within 30 seconds, and swipe away. Viewer count drops, the algorithm stops pushing the stream, and the session dies.

## Preparation before going live

**Technical:**
- Light pointed at your face and the product (a basic ring light is enough)
- Stable internet — use a wired connection if possible
- Clear audio with no echo — a cheap lapel mic beats a phone mic
- Tidy, non-distracting background

**Inventory:**
- Arrange products in the exact order you will present them
- Prepare a **hook product** (great price, limited quantity) to pull people in early
- Verify stock levels and that listed prices are correct

**Content:**
- Write out the product order + price + key selling point for each
- Prepare answers for your five most common questions

## A time-blocked 60-minute script

| Minutes | Goal | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **0-10** | Pull people in | Hook product at a striking price, repeatedly tease "a big deal is coming" |
| **10-25** | Hold attention | Hero products, thorough demos, tell the product story |
| **25-35** | Peak | Minute-limited deals, count down remaining stock |
| **35-50** | Handle objections | Answer comments, compare variants, advise on size and color |
| **50-60** | Final close | Recap every deal, warn that it is ending, close orders |

> Every **5-7 minutes**, repeat: who you are, what you are selling, the price, and how to order. Viewers join continuously — they have no idea what you said earlier.

## 4 techniques to hold viewers

**1. Anchor with an upcoming deal**
"In 5 minutes I'm opening the $4 deal, stick around" — gives viewers a concrete reason not to swipe.

**2. Say commenters' names**
"Lan, you asked about size L, right?" — feeling acknowledged keeps people watching noticeably longer.

**3. Demonstrate, don't just describe**
Try it on, pour water on the waterproof fabric, bend the product. Action persuades more than words.

**4. Create scarcity — but make it true**
"12 left" only works if there really are 12 left. Lie once and you lose customers permanently.

## 5 mistakes that tank your viewer count

1. **Long silences** while hunting for stock or reading comments — narrate while you work.
2. **Unclear pricing** that forces viewers to ask repeatedly.
3. **Ignoring comments** — a person who feels ignored leaves immediately.
4. **Pure selling** with no entertaining or useful content in between.
5. **Ending abruptly** — give a 5-minute warning and announce your next stream.

## After the stream: the part most people skip

- **Confirm orders within the first 2 hours** — customers still remember, and cancellations drop sharply.
- **Clip the best moments** into short videos to promote your next session.
- **Record the numbers**: when viewership peaked, which products sold, which lines drove orders.

That data sheet is what makes each stream better than the last.

## Where to start

If you have never streamed, do not set a revenue target for your first session. **The only goal: stay live for the full 60 minutes without quitting early.**

Do five sessions consistently before analyzing the numbers. Livestreaming is a skill that improves with repetitions, not with theory.
