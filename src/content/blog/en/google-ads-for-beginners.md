If Facebook Ads is finding customers in a crowd, Google Ads is **standing exactly where customers are already looking for you**. Someone typing "aircon repair district 7" needs that service right now — existing demand no other channel replaces.

But for that same reason, running it wrong burns money fast.

## Which campaign type should you start with?

Google offers many campaign types, but beginners **should start with Search only**:

| Type | Good for | Should beginners run it? |
|---|---|---|
| **Search** | People actively seeking a solution | ✅ Start here |
| Display | Awareness, remarketing | ⚠️ After you have data |
| Video (YouTube) | Brand storytelling | ⚠️ Needs strong video |
| Performance Max | Cross-channel automation | ❌ Very easy to lose control |

> Performance Max sounds appealing because "Google handles everything", but without conversion data it usually spends money in ways you cannot explain.

## Get your account structure right from day one

Many people dump every keyword into one ad group — that is why their ads feel irrelevant and their bids get expensive.

A proper structure:

```
Campaign: Aircon services
├── Ad group: Aircon repair
│     └── keywords: aircon repair, ac repair...
├── Ad group: Aircon cleaning
│     └── keywords: aircon cleaning, ac servicing...
└── Ad group: Aircon installation
      └── keywords: aircon installation, ac install...
```

The rule: **one theme per ad group**, and the ad copy must echo that group's keywords. Google scores quality on relevance — a higher score means a cheaper cost per click.

## Keyword match types: where money leaks

This is what beginners misunderstand most:

| Match type | How to write it | Shows when |
|---|---|---|
| Broad | `aircon repair` | Any search Google considers related |
| Phrase | `"aircon repair"` | Contains that phrase in order |
| Exact | `[aircon repair]` | Closely matches that exact phrase |

Beginners should start with **Phrase and Exact**. Broad match without data will show your ads on all kinds of irrelevant searches.

## Negative keywords: non-optional

This is the list of terms you do **not** want to appear for. For an aircon repair service, add these immediately:

`free`, `diy`, `how to fix`, `jobs`, `training`, `cheapest`

Every week, open the **Search terms** report to see what people actually typed, then add more negatives. This is the fastest way to save budget in Google Ads.

## Writing effective ad copy

Search ads consist of headlines and descriptions. The principles:

1. **Echo the keyword** in the first headline — people see exactly what they searched.
2. **State your differentiator** in the second headline: "On site within 60 minutes", "6-month warranty".
3. **Add specific numbers** — figures are always more credible than adjectives.
4. **Use every extension**: call, sitelinks, callouts. They are free and increase your ad's screen space.

## The landing page decides half the result

A common mistake: advertising "aircon cleaning" but sending traffic to the homepage. Visitors have to hunt — and most leave.

The rule: **each ad group points to a page about exactly that topic**, with a call or message button near the top.

## Budget and expectations

- Start small: enough for roughly **20-30 clicks per day** per campaign.
- **Do not adjust daily.** Wait at least 7-14 days before drawing conclusions.
- The first week is for **collecting data**, not for profit.

## Before you launch

Ask yourself: *when someone clicks the ad, what will they see and what will they do next?*

If you cannot answer clearly, fix the landing page before spending a single dollar on ads.
