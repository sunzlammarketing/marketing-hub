A familiar scene: you open your laptop, stare at a blank screen, and ask "what do I post today?". Two hours later you publish something mediocre.

The problem is not a lack of ideas — it is that **you are generating ideas at the wrong moment**. The fix: sit down once and plan the whole month.

## The 4-bucket formula

Stop thinking "what do I write today". Think in **buckets of purpose**:

| Bucket | Purpose | Suggested share |
|---|---|---|
| **Value** | Teach something useful | 40% |
| **Connection** | Stories, behind the scenes, opinions | 30% |
| **Proof** | Reviews, case studies, customer results | 20% |
| **Sales** | Product intros, calls to buy | 10% |

This ratio keeps your page from **turning into a marketplace**. People stay for Value and Connection, then buy because of Proof and Sales.

> If 80% of your posts are sales posts, falling engagement is inevitable — both the algorithm and your audience notice.

## A 90-minute planning process

**Step 1 — Brain dump (20 min).** Write 30-40 raw ideas without judging them. Easiest sources:
- Questions customers keep asking
- Common misconceptions in your industry
- Mistakes you have made yourself
- Comments under a competitor's viral post

**Step 2 — Sort into buckets (15 min).** Tag each idea with one of the four buckets.

**Step 3 — Schedule (25 min).** Spread them by ratio. For a 3-posts-per-week cadence:
- Tuesday: Value post
- Thursday: Connection post
- Saturday: Proof or Sales post (alternating)

**Step 4 — Write the hooks first (30 min).** Write only the **opening hook** for all 12 posts. Once the hook exists, writing the body goes much faster.

## A concrete one-week sample

| Day | Bucket | Idea |
|---|---|---|
| Tue | Value | "3 headline mistakes that make people scroll past" |
| Thu | Connection | "My first ever order and the lesson I never forgot" |
| Sat | Proof | "How Hoa increased orders 40% by fixing one thing" |

Only three posts, but three different purposes — far stronger than seven posts that all sound the same.

## One idea, many formats

Do not let an idea be used only once. One long article can become:

- 1 full blog post
- 3 short posts (one per section)
- 1 short summary video
- 1 checklist graphic
- 1 discussion question

This cuts your creative workload to roughly **one fifth**.

## 3 scheduling mistakes

1. **Overambitious calendars** — planning 7 posts a week then quitting in week two. Three consistent posts beat that.
2. **No room for timely content** — leave 1-2 open slots each week for trends or industry news.
3. **Never reviewing the numbers** — at month end, check which posts performed so you can do more of that next month.

## Start today

Give yourself **30 minutes** right now: open a notes app and write down 20 questions customers have asked you.

Those are 20 Value posts — nearly two months of content, and guaranteed to be relevant because your customers asked for them.
