Have you noticed that with some brands you recognize them from the caption alone, without seeing the logo? That comes from **brand voice**.

On the other hand, many shops post formally today, in slang tomorrow, and copy a competitor's tone the day after — leaving customers with no sense of who the brand is.

## Voice vs. tone

- **Voice** is the brand's fixed personality — it does not change.
- **Tone** is how that personality shows up in a given situation — it does change.

Example: a brand whose voice is *friendly and straightforward*. Launching a product, it sounds excited; apologizing to a customer, it sounds serious — but it stays friendly and straightforward throughout.

## Define your voice with 3 questions

**1. If the brand were a person, what kind of person?**
Write down 3-4 adjectives. For example: *approachable · practical · no-fluff · lightly humorous*.

**2. Who does the brand talk to, and in what role?**
A teacher? A friend a few steps ahead? An expert? This role decides how you address people.

**3. How would we NEVER sound?**
Just as important. For example: *no grandiose language, no fear-mongering, no absolute promises*.

## A voice table: the most practical tool

Do not write a 20-page document nobody reads. A table like this is enough:

| We ARE | We are NOT | Write this | Instead of |
|---|---|---|---|
| Approachable | Overly casual | "Give this a try" | "Yo just do this" |
| Straightforward | Harsh | "This approach falls short because..." | "That is completely wrong" |
| Realistic | Over-promising | "Most see improvement in 4-6 weeks" | "100% instant results" |
| Clear | Condescending | "Put simply..." | "You probably won't get this" |

The **"Write this / Instead of"** columns are the most valuable part — they turn an abstract idea into something usable today.

## Consistency in how you address people

How you address your reader sets the relationship immediately:

- **First person, casual** ("I / you"): close and equal, good for sharing knowledge
- **Corporate** ("we / our customers"): formal, fits B2B and premium services
- **Shop voice** ("the shop / you"): friendly retail style

Pick **one style** and keep it across every channel. This is the most common consistency mistake I see.

## Quick check before publishing

Read the draft again and ask:

1. If the brand name were covered, could a reader tell it was us?
2. Does it contain anything from the "We are NOT" column?
3. Does it feel awkward read aloud? (Awkward usually means off-voice.)

## Start today

Open your last 5 posts and read them back to back. If they sound like 5 different people wrote them, it is time to build a voice table.

Just a 4-row table like the one above, pinned in your team's shared doc — you will see consistency improve within the first week.
