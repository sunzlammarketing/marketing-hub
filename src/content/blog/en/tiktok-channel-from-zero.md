TikTok is one of the rare platforms where a brand-new account can still go viral purely on content quality. But "getting lucky" is not a strategy. Here is a systematic 90-day roadmap.

## Understand how distribution works

TikTok does not care how many followers you have. Each video is pushed to a small group first, then the platform uses signals to decide whether to push further:

- **Watch-through rate** — the most important signal.
- **Rewatches**.
- **Engagement**: likes, comments, shares, saves.

> So the number one goal of every video is simple: keep the viewer to the last second.

## Month 1: Find your niche and format

- Pick a **niche narrow enough to stand out** (e.g. "Excel tips for office workers" rather than "technology").
- Test 3–4 formats: quick tutorials, storytelling, reviews, trend participation.
- Post consistently — **one video per day** — to gather data.

## Month 2: Double down on what works

After 30 days, review your numbers:

1. Which video had the highest watch time?
2. Which topic got the most comments?
3. Which opening hook held attention best?

Then **do more** of what is working and drop what is not. This is when the channel starts to accelerate.

## Month 3: Build community and convert

- Reply to comments with video responses (TikTok favors this feature).
- Ask for follows naturally at the end of genuinely valuable videos.
- Start guiding viewers: to your bio, your product, your other platforms.

## 3 mistakes that keep channels small

- **Weak hooks**: the first 3 seconds give no reason to keep watching.
- **Inconsistent posting**: the algorithm "forgets" you.
- **Chasing trends off-niche**: you go viral but keep none of the viewers.

Growing a TikTok channel is like going to the gym: results come from **consistency and data-driven adjustment**, not from one miracle video.
