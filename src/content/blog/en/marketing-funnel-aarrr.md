Many people do marketing in fragments: run ads today, post tomorrow, send an email the day after — with no overall picture. The **marketing funnel** lets you see the entire journey and spot which link is weak.

## What is a marketing funnel?

Picture a funnel: many people hear about you at the top, but only a portion reach the bottom and become customers. The job of marketing is to **reduce leakage at each stage**.

## The AARRR model — the "pirate funnel"

A simple but practical model with five stages:

1. **Acquisition** — how do people find out about you? (SEO, ads, social)
2. **Activation** — is the first experience good enough to make them stay? (landing page, signup, trial)
3. **Retention** — do they come back? (email, content, customer care)
4. **Revenue** — have they paid yet? (sales, upsell)
5. **Referral** — do they recommend you to others?

## Why this model helps

Because it forces you to **measure each stage** and find the bottleneck:

| Symptom | Weak stage | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| Lots of traffic, few signups | Activation | Improve the landing page |
| Customers buy once and vanish | Retention | Build a nurture email flow |
| Many leads, few orders | Revenue | Review pricing and offer |

## How to apply it

You do not need to optimize all five stages at once. Instead:

1. Draw your own funnel.
2. Estimate the numbers at each stage (even rough guesses are fine at first).
3. Find the stage **leaking the most** and fix that first.

> Pouring more people into a leaky funnel only makes you lose money faster. Patch the holes before increasing the budget.

Once you think in funnels, every scattered marketing activity finds its place in a system — and you know exactly where to focus your effort.
