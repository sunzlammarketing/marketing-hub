You write polished content but your posts still get silence? The problem is usually not the product — it is the **persuasion structure**. And AIDA is the oldest, simplest formula to fix that.

AIDA guides the reader through four psychological steps: **Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**.

## A — Attention

The first 3 seconds decide whether someone stops or scrolls. Your opening line must hit a real pain point or spark curiosity:

> ❌ "We sell sunscreen."
>
> ✅ "Wearing sunscreen every day but your skin still darkens? You might be using the wrong type."

## I — Interest

Once you have attention, nurture it with information that is directly relevant to the reader:

- Explain the root cause of the problem.
- Offer a fresh perspective.
- Tell a short story they see themselves in.

## D — Desire

This is where you move from "problem" to "your product is the solution". The key: **sell benefits, not features**.

| Feature | Benefit (what they actually want) |
|---|---|
| SPF 50+, PA++++ | Skin stays even-toned even after a full day outdoors |
| Lightweight texture | No greasy shine, makeup sits smoothly on top |
| Natural extracts | Peace of mind for sensitive skin |

Then add **proof**: reviews from past customers, before-and-after photos, sales numbers.

## A — Action

Do not leave the reader hesitating. A good CTA is **clear + creates urgency**:

> "Message us today to get **20% off** — available for the first 30 customers this week only."

## How to apply it

Next time you write a sales post, check it against this list:

1. Does the first line make people stop scrolling?
2. Does the middle speak to what the customer actually cares about?
3. Have I stated **benefits** instead of just listing features?
4. Is the CTA clear and easy to act on?

AIDA is only the skeleton. What brings it to life is **genuinely understanding your customer**. The better you know them, the more your content lands.
