Many people think email is "outdated". In reality it remains the channel with the **highest return on investment** in marketing — because you own your list and do not depend on any platform's algorithm.

The key is not sending more email, but building an **automated funnel** that sends the right thing at the right time.

## Step 1: Give people a reason to hand over their email

Nobody gives away their email for free. You need a **lead magnet** — something valuable in exchange:

- A useful ebook or checklist.
- A discount code for the first order.
- A mini-course delivered by email.

The rule: your lead magnet must solve **one specific, small problem** for your audience.

## Step 2: The welcome sequence

This is your most important sequence — a new subscriber is at peak interest. A solid four-email structure:

1. **Email 1 (immediately)**: deliver the promised gift + welcome.
2. **Email 2 (after 1 day)**: tell your brand story, build connection.
3. **Email 3 (after 3 days)**: share value, solve a pain point.
4. **Email 4 (after 5 days)**: introduce your product with a light offer.

> Welcome emails typically get 3–4× the open rate of regular emails. Do not waste that golden moment.

## Step 3: Segmentation

Do not send the same email to everyone. Segment by:

- Behavior: purchased / not purchased / abandoned cart.
- Interest: which topics they click on.
- Engagement level: "hot" versus "cold" subscribers.

The more relevant the email, the higher your open and click rates.

## Step 4: Measure and optimize

| Metric | Healthy benchmark |
|---|---|
| Open rate | 25–40% |
| Click-through rate | 2–5% |
| Unsubscribe rate | < 0.5% |

Test your **subject lines** regularly — they have the single biggest impact on open rates.

## An important note

Always get permission before adding someone to your list, and **always include an unsubscribe link**. Building long-term trust matters far more than a handful of short-term orders. A quality email list is the most valuable asset you can own.
