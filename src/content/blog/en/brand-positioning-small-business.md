For a small business, a brand is not a beautiful logo or an elaborate identity kit. A brand is **the feeling and thought that appears in a customer's mind when they hear your name**. And that starts with **positioning**.

## What is brand positioning?

Positioning is a clear answer to one question: *"Why should a customer choose you over every other option?"*

If you cannot answer that yourself, your customers will not either.

## The one-sentence positioning formula

Fill in this template:

> For **[target customer]**, **[your brand]** is the **[product category]** that helps **[distinctive benefit]**, unlike **[competitors]** because **[reason to believe]**.

Example:

> For busy office workers, *Cơm Nhà* is a lunch delivery service that makes healthy daily eating effortless, unlike ordinary eateries because every menu is designed by a nutritionist.

## 3 common positioning mistakes

1. **Trying to please everyone** → nobody feels it is made for them.
2. **Competing on low price** → there is always someone cheaper, and your margins erode.
3. **Vague positioning** ("good quality, reasonable price") → everyone says that; it creates no distinction.

## Finding your differentiator (USP)

Your differentiator does not have to be the product itself. It can come from:

- **Experience**: customer care that exceeds expectations.
- **Story**: your origin, values, and the people behind it.
- **Specialization**: serving one very narrow niche better than anyone.
- **Delivery**: faster, more convenient, more personalized.

## Consistency is the real power

Once you have positioning, express it **consistently** at every touchpoint: how you write content, your tone of voice, your visuals, how you reply to customers. That repeated consistency is what carves your name into a customer's mind.

> A strong brand is not the one most people know — it is the one the **right** people remember and trust.
