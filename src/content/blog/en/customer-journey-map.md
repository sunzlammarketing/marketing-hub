You run ads, people visit, and then... they vanish. Usually not because the product is bad, but because **some step in the journey made them stop** — and you do not know which one.

A customer journey map shows you exactly where that leak is.

## What is a customer journey?

It is the full chain of interactions from the moment someone **has never heard of you** to the point they **come back for a third purchase**. It is far longer than "see ad → click buy".

Five basic stages:

| Stage | What they are doing | What they need from you |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | Just realized they have a problem | Content that explains the problem |
| **Consideration** | Comparing solutions | Comparisons, reviews, proof |
| **Decision** | Choosing where to buy | Clear pricing, reassuring policies |
| **Usage** | Using the product | Guides, fast support |
| **Loyalty** | Considering coming back | Follow-up care, returning-customer offers |

## How to map it in 45 minutes

You do not need expensive tools. Take one sheet of paper, make five columns for the five stages, then fill in four rows:

1. **Actions** — what does the customer do at this step?
2. **Thoughts & feelings** — what are they worried or unsure about?
3. **Touchpoints** — where do they meet you? (ads, website, messages, packaging...)
4. **Barriers** — what could make them stop?

> Do not just imagine it. Base it on the messages customers actually sent, the reviews they left, and the questions that keep repeating in your inbox.

## Example: an online cosmetics shop

**Consideration stage:**
- *Action*: reads reviews, asks friends, checks the shop's profile
- *Thought*: "Is this actually authentic?"
- *Touchpoint*: comments under posts, direct messages
- *Barrier*: **cannot find proof of authenticity**

Once you see that barrier, the fix becomes obvious: post photos of import invoices, unboxing videos, distribution paperwork — and pin them to the top of the page.

## Find the leak with numbers

Pair your map with a few simple figures:

- How many saw the ad → how many visited the page?
- How many visited → how many sent a message?
- How many messaged → how many placed an order?
- How many bought → how many bought again?

Whichever ratio **drops the hardest** is what you should fix first. Pouring more ad money in while people drop off at the messaging step only loses money faster.

## 3 common leaks

1. **Slow inbox replies** — someone asks at 9pm; if you reply the next morning, they have already bought elsewhere.
2. **Hiding the price** — forcing people to "message for price" makes many leave immediately.
3. **Silence after the sale** — no thank-you message or usage guide, so customers never remember to return.

## What to do this week

Pick the **single stage** you suspect leaks the most. Write down three customer barriers there, then fix one of them.

One week, one leak. After two months you will have a journey noticeably smoother than your competitors'.
