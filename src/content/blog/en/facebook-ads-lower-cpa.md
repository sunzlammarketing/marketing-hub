Facebook ad prices rise every year, and performance slips quickly without regular optimization. The good news: most accounts are burning money in places that can be fixed. Below are the five levers with the biggest impact on **CPA (cost per conversion)**.

## 1. Start with the audience, not the creative

The classic mistake is pouring money into an audience that is far too broad. Prioritize instead:

- **Custom Audiences**: people who already engaged, visited your site, or watched your videos.
- **Lookalike Audiences**: built from your existing buyers (1–3%).
- Only use **cold interest-based targeting** once you have data for Facebook to learn from.

## 2. Feed the algorithm enough data

Each ad set needs roughly **50 conversions per week** to exit the learning phase. So:

- Do not split your budget across dozens of tiny ad sets.
- Do not edit constantly — each significant change **resets the learning phase**.

> Being patient through the first 3–4 days is the hardest part, and also the most important.

## 3. Creative is king

Once your audience and budget are stable, **the ad creative drives about 70% of the result**:

- Test at least 3–5 creatives with genuinely different angles (not just a color change).
- The first 3 seconds of a video must hold attention.
- Try multiple formats: single image, short video, carousel.

## 4. Read the right metrics

Do not just look at likes. Track:

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| Link CTR | Is the creative compelling? |
| CPC | Cost per click |
| CPA | Cost per actual conversion |
| ROAS | Revenue per ad dollar spent |

Low CTR → fix the creative. High CTR but high CPA → the problem is your **landing page or pricing**.

## 5. Optimize the landing page, do not blame the ad

Ads only bring people to the door. The landing page closes the sale:

- Fast load times (especially on mobile).
- The landing page message **matches** the ad promise.
- Clear CTA and short forms.

## Summary

Lowering CPA does not come from one magic trick — it comes from **continuously optimizing the whole system**: right audience → enough data → strong creative → smooth landing page. Pick your weakest lever right now and improve that first.
