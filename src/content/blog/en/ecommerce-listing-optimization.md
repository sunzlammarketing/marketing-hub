Many sellers pour money into ads to drive people to their store, but overlook the more important question: **once they arrive, how many actually buy?**

With the same 1,000 views, a store converting at 1% makes 10 orders while a store at 3% makes 30 — **without spending another cent on ads**. These are the levers worth pulling first.

## 1. Images: decided in two seconds

On a marketplace, shoppers scroll fast. Images are the only thing that stops them.

- **Cover image**: bright background, product filling 70-80% of the frame, minimal text overlay (many platforms suppress reach on text-heavy images).
- **Images 2-5**: close-ups of material, size comparison (next to a familiar object), real in-use photos.
- **Final image**: warranty and return information — handle worries before they are asked.

> Quick test: open your listing on a **phone** and shrink the screen. If you can still tell what the product is at small size, the image works.

## 2. Titles: written for humans and for marketplace search

A safe formula:

> **[Brand] + [Product name] + [Key attribute] + [Spec/Size] + [Secondary keyword]**

Example:
- ❌ "Nice cheap men's t-shirt hot trend 2026 mega sale"
- ✅ "Men's 100% cotton oversized t-shirt, drop shoulder — unisex tee, multiple colors"

The second title contains **words customers actually type** (cotton, oversized, drop shoulder, unisex) instead of meaningless filler like "hot trend" and "mega sale".

## 3. Descriptions: answer questions before they are asked

A description structure that reads well on mobile:

1. **Three bullet points of key benefits** (not specs)
2. **Detailed specifications** (dimensions, material, weight)
3. **Sizing / usage guidance**
4. **Return and warranty policy**
5. **Packaging and delivery commitments**

Every question customers frequently message you about equals one line you should add. It lifts conversion and saves you time answering messages.

## 4. Pricing and promotions: psychology without discounting yourself to death

| Tactic | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Bundles of 2-3 items | Raises order value without cutting unit price |
| Free-shipping threshold | Customers add items to reach it |
| Discount code for next order | Brings them back a second time |
| Charm pricing (199k vs 200k) | Feels meaningfully cheaper |

Avoid continuous deep discounts — customers adapt and **only buy during sales**, eroding your margins.

## 5. Reviews: your store's most valuable asset

Products with **photo reviews** convert noticeably better than those with star ratings alone.

How to earn reviews naturally:
- Include a handwritten thank-you note in the parcel with a gentle review request.
- Offer a small discount code for their next purchase after they review.
- **Always respond to negative reviews** politely with a resolution — future readers judge how you handle problems more than the problem itself.

> Do not buy fake reviews. Platforms detect them increasingly well, and the risk of a store ban far outweighs any short-term gain.

## 6. Measurement: 4 numbers to check weekly

- **Product views** → effectiveness of visibility and ads
- **Conversion rate** → quality of the listing (images, price, reviews)
- **Average order value** → effectiveness of bundles and upsells
- **Cancellation and return rate** → product quality and description honesty

If views are high but conversion is low, the problem is the **listing**, not the ads. This is the most common misdiagnosis that leads people to burn money pointlessly.

## What to do this week

Pick your **single best-selling product** and do three things: reshoot a bright, clear cover image, rewrite the title using the formula above, and add the return policy to the description.

Just one product — then compare the numbers after two weeks. Once you see the effect, you will know exactly what to scale.
